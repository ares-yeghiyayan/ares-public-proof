# Weekly Note 002: Review Before Claim

## Public-safe summary

This week tightened a simple public rule: a claim should wait until the route, boundary, and proof artifact are easy to inspect.

The public surface is stronger when it shows review discipline before it asks for belief.

## What improved

- The profile route became easier to inspect.
- Public proof navigation became cleaner.
- Review material became easier to find.
- The public layer stayed short instead of becoming noisy.

## What stayed consistent

- private work stayed private
- public notes stayed high-level
- claims stayed behind evidence
- boundaries stayed visible
- changes stayed small and reviewable

## What to tighten next

Keep future public notes useful but restrained. The next signal should be another small artifact that makes review easier, not a larger claim.

## Public signal

```text
route -> boundary -> review -> claim restraint
```

## Next tightening step

Before adding broader public language, check whether the existing proof route already says enough.

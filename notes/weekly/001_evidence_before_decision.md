# Weekly Note 001: Evidence Before Decision

## Public-safe summary

This week tightened the proof rhythm around one simple rule: important decisions should not start from claims. They should start from reviewable evidence, clear boundaries, and small artifacts that can be checked.

## What improved

- The public story stayed focused on discipline rather than hype.
- The build rhythm stayed small enough to review.
- Boundary language stayed visible before any claim language.
- The proof trail became easier to explain without exposing sensitive work.

## What stayed private

- internal architecture
- operating details
- non-public research
- sensitive strategy
- implementation mechanics
- customer or market material

## Public signal

```text
claim -> pause -> evidence -> review -> boundary -> next small artifact
```

## Next tightening step

Keep future public notes short, safe, and useful for a reviewer who wants to see consistency rather than promises.

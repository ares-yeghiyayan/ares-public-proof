# Public Proof Rhythm

## Purpose

This note defines a simple public-safe rhythm for showing learning discipline and careful software-building without exposing private research or sensitive implementation details.

Public proof should show how work is organized, reviewed, documented, and improved over time. It should not reveal private architecture, internal naming systems, workflow mechanics, market strategy, customer assumptions, or proprietary methods.

## Rhythm

A useful public proof rhythm is small and repeatable:

1. Open a clear issue before doing the work.
2. Make one narrow improvement on a branch.
3. Keep the change easy to review.
4. Run the available checks.
5. Merge only when the boundary remains safe.
6. Record what improved without making broad claims.

## What This Proves

This kind of public work can demonstrate:

- consistency
- documentation discipline
- review discipline
- safety awareness
- respect for scope
- ability to ship small improvements

## Safety Boundary

Public notes should remain high-level. They should avoid private designs, internal names, detailed workflows, sensitive assumptions, prompts, proprietary methods, financial advice, performance claims, or production readiness claims.

## Next Improvement

The next public-safe improvement should add another small note, checklist, or quality check that strengthens the public workflow without expanding the exposed surface area.
